About Malitbog

History

LEGEND OF MALITBOG

In the early 1700s, Malitbog was founded by a Datu and his three right-hand men. The Datu's daughter fell in love with a young harvester, which led to a series of tragic events, including the deaths of the daughter and her lover due to the Datu's anger. The Datu, filled with remorse, named the town Malitbog in her memory. The Saguilongan and Kilem families also played significant roles in the town's development.

Culture

Araw ng Malitbog (Kadisnon Festival) One of the highlights of our cultural calendar is the annual Araw ng Malitbog, also known as the Kadisnon Festival, celebrated every December 14th to 16th. This vibrant festival brings the community together in joyous festivities.

Street Dancing

Witness the energetic performances as participants, dressed in traditional attire, dance through the streets, celebrating our heritage.

Float Competition

Marvel at the creatively decorated floats that parade through the town, each telling a story of Malitbog’s culture and history.

1KM Ginanggang Cookout

Experience the unique 1km Ginanggang Cookout, where locals and visitors alike grill bananas over an open flame, creating a delightful treat.

Boxing Kawayan

Witness the thrilling Boxing Kawayan event, where participants use bamboo sticks in a traditional martial arts display, showcasing skill, agility, and cultural heritage. It's a captivating experience that reflects the strength and spirit of our community.

Geography and Demographics

Malitbog is located at the northernmost part of the province, between parallel 8°25'35" and 8°36'8" North Latitude and Meridians 124°49'39" and 125°11'39" East Longitude. Malitbog is a landlocked municipality bounded on the north by the municipality of Claveria and on the western side by Tagoloan – both of which are part of Misamis Oriental. Bounding it on its southern part are the municipalities of Manolo Fortich and Impasugong of Bukidnon Province while on its eastern side is the municipality of Esperanza, Agusan del Sur.

The Municipality is about 40 kilometres (25 mi) from the regional capital – Cagayan de Oro City, and about 2½ hours ride from the Provincial Capital, Malaybalay City. It is reachable via Tagoloan and is approximately 2 hours ride from the City of Cagayan de Oro through secondary and first-class road types (gravel and concrete pavement).

The climate in Malitbog is tropical, characterized by significant rainfall throughout the year. The town experiences a lush environment due to its consistent rain, making it a haven for agriculture. Additionally, Malitbog boasts diverse topography, with rolling hills, fertile plains, and scenic rivers.

Visitors to Malitbog are often mesmerized by its natural beauty and the rich biodiversity it supports, making it an ideal destination for eco-tourism and agriculture-related industries.
